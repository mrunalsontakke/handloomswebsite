Why Handloom of India ?

Indian handloom sector is an age-old industry employing the rural people at a large level. They have separate space in the Indian economy. The handloom industry is considered the largest cottage industry in the country. The sector is leading the second position after agriculture in providing employment. They are producing eco-friendly products. Raw materials are taken from agriculture, therefore, the continuous market for agricultural produce. This shows the dexterity and artistic ability of weavers to produce attractive products. They are changing their products based on market needs and aspirations. About Handloom Sector

Indian handloom industry is deeply rooted in the sociocultural traditions with a rich heritage of skills and talent that needs to be preserved, perpetuated and promoted. India has regionally distinct varieties of handlooms like Ikats from Andhra Pradesh and Orissa, tie and dye from Gujarat and Rajasthan, brocades from Banaras, Pashmina from Kashmir and many more.
